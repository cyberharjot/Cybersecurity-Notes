# 🔐 Introduction to Cybersecurity

## 📌 What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is basically about protecting systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access or attacks.

In today’s time, almost everything is online — banking, communication, personal data — so keeping it secure becomes really important.

It’s not just about hackers, it’s also about making sure data stays safe, private, and unchanged.

---

## 🌐 What is Cyberspace?

Cyberspace refers to the digital environment created by interconnected systems and networks.

In simple terms, it’s the space where all online activities happen — websites, apps, games, servers, communication, everything.

Whenever we use the internet, we are interacting with cyberspace.

---

## ⚠️ What is Cybercrime?

Cybercrime is any illegal activity that involves a computer, network, or the internet.

It can be done to steal data, damage systems, or misuse someone’s identity.

### Examples:

* Hacking into accounts
* Phishing attacks
* Identity theft
* Spreading malware

With increasing digital use, cybercrime is also growing rapidly.

---

## 🧠 Important Terminologies

### 🔹 Vulnerability

A vulnerability is a weakness in a system that can be exploited by an attacker.

---

### 🔹 Threat

A threat is something that has the potential to cause harm to a system or data.

---

### 🔹 Exploit

An exploit is a technique or method used to take advantage of a vulnerability.

---

### 🔹 Malware

Malware is malicious software designed to damage or disrupt systems.

Examples include:

* Virus
* Worm
* Trojan

---

### 🔹 Firewall

A firewall is a security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on rules.

---

### 🔹 Encryption

Encryption is the process of converting data into a secure format so that unauthorized users cannot read it.

---

## 🎯 Summary

Cybersecurity plays a crucial role in protecting digital information and systems.

As dependency on technology increases, understanding these basic concepts becomes important.
